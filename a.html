Types of Sea

Seas are mainly classified into three types: inland seas, marginal seas, and open seas.
Inland seas are almost completely surrounded by land and connected to the ocean by narrow waterways, such as the Mediterranean Sea.
Marginal seas are partly enclosed by islands or peninsulas and are located along the edges of oceans, like the South China Sea.
Open seas are large bodies of water that have a wide connection with the ocean and are not enclosed by land, such as the Arabian Sea.
Each type of sea has unique geographical features and supports diverse marine life.
